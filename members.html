Current Members

I completed both my Masters in Science and Doctorate in Medicine degrees at Tufts University School of Medicine. While at Tufts, I participated in the Research Concentration Track, which allowed me to perform basic research in HIV immunology and cancer cell biology with the HIV Vaccine Trials Network and the Howard Hughes Medical Institute, respectively. After medical school, I continued my training in radiation oncology at Columbia University Medical Center, where I developed an interest in brain tumor immunology and CNS-directed radiotherapy. During residency, I conducted research investigating the impact of ultra-high dose-rate radiation on the immune microenvironment of diffuse midline glioma. Moving forward, I am interested in dissecting mechanisms of anti-tumor immune responses in the context of novel radiation and immune-based modalities.

Fun facts: I speak four languages and enjoy writing creative fiction in two of them during my spare time. My hobbies include: playing harmonica, spoken word poetry and basketball.
